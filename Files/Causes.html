The struggles that prevent people from receiving a good education are just as serious as its effects. Countries with weak economies and weak education systems often remain stuck in a cycle that is hard to break without any other external support. A strong education system requires a stable economy, and a strong economy depends on quality education. This makes ensuring access to education for everyone one of the most important global challenges. In 2023, adult illiteracy rates remained high, with nearly 32 percent in Sub-Saharan Africa and 25 percent in South Asia who cannot read or write, emphasizing the need for better educational opportunities to break this cycle and promote development. Below are some common causes of this lack of education:

  1. Discrimination
  2. Lack of schools
  3. Poor conditions
  4. Poverty
