1 Education For All

Learn More About the Sustainable Development Goal 4 by Browsing through this Site!





Since 2000, significant efforts have been made toward achieving universal primary education. The global number of children out of school has been reduced by about half. By 2015, the overall enrollment rate in developing regions has reached 91%. Moreover, literacy rates have soared, and more girls are attending school than ever before, showing remarkable progress. However, challenges continue to disrupt developing regions due to high poverty rates, armed conflicts, and other crises. Ongoing conflicts in Western Asia and North Africa have led to an increase in the number of children not attending school. Sub-Saharan Africa, despite facing significant obstacles, has made the most development in primary school enrollment among all regions.

Why is it so important?

Fixing the lack of good education is really important because it helps make things fairer for everyone, especially for people who don’t have many opportunities. Good education helps people get out of poverty and have better lives. It also helps the economy grow because educated people can come up with new ideas and work better. Education gives people the knowledge and skills to make smart choices about their lives and their communities. It also helps keep peace and understanding between different groups by teaching tolerance and how to work together. With big problems like climate change and new technology, having educated people is important so we can find solutions and adapt. In the end, helping improve education is the best way to create fairer and better societies.
